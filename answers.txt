Q3: What is the difference between merging and rebasing?

Answer: Merging creates a merge commit that combines two branches' 
histories, preserving all previous commits. Rebasing, however, moves or 
rewrites commits from one branch onto another, creating a linear history. 
You should use merging to preserve full context and rebasing to streamline 
the commit history without merge commits.
